About Bo Rodda

Assistant Professor of Experiential Media and Design at Illinois Tech

Exploring the intersection of design, technology, and storytelling

Bo Rodda is an internationally exhibited designer and co-founder of ChiLab Design Studio. His research and practice explore the intersection of design, emerging technology, game design and data-driven storytelling.

He leads cross-sector collaborations that bridge the arts, science, and innovation. His work has been supported by the NSF, Infosys, United Airlines, Wilson Sporting Goods, among many others. He engages audiences through both public exhibition and applied research.

Education

Research & Projects

Groundbreaking work at the intersection of technology, design, and urban systems

LakeSIM

Urban Simulation and Planning Platform developed in collaboration with University of Chicago and Argonne National Laboratory.

2015 - Present

Lead architect of a data-driven tool designed to connect urban design concepts with scientific modeling. Funded in part by the NSF, and featured in ISOCARP Review.

Senior personnel on a groundbreaking urban sensing project co-developed with Argonne National Laboratory and the University of Chicago.

The Array of Things

Contributed to sensor design, public engagement strategies, and educational workshops.

2013 - 2017

Chicago City Model

Dynamic Data Projection Mapping for the Chicago Architecture Foundation's City of Big Data exhibition.

2012 - 2014

Created a projection system mapping real-time urban data onto a 3D model of the city, serving as technical advisor for the exhibition.
